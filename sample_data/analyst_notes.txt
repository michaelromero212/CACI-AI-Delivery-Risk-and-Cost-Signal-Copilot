POSITIVE INDICATORS

The data migration workstream continues to perform well. The 99.7% accuracy

================================================================================
AREAS OF CONCERN
================================================================================

1. INTEGRATION WORKSTREAM

The legacy API situation is more concerning than the status report conveys.
In conversations with Tom's team, I'm hearing that the reverse engineering
effort is uncovering significant technical debt that wasn't visible during
planning. Some of the mainframe code dates to 1987 and lacks any documentation.

I recommend we prepare a contingency plan for a 30-day slip, not just 10 days.
The current estimate feels optimistic given what I've observed.

2. CHANGE REQUEST MANAGEMENT

The CCB backlog is growing faster than anticipated. 15 new requests this month
represents a 3x increase over our planning assumptions. While individually
manageable, the cumulative impact on requirements traceability is concerning.

I've started drafting a scope impact analysis to present at the January CCB.

3. KNOWLEDGE CONCENTRATION

Beyond the personnel backup issue noted in the status report, I'm seeing a
concerning pattern with Jennifer Park. She is the only person who fully
understands the data transformation logic; if she were unavailable, we would
struggle to troubleshoot migration issues.

Recommend scheduling explicit knowledge transfer sessions in January.

4. VENDOR RELATIONSHIP

While the hardware delay is noted as "no impact," I've heard grumbling from
the vendor about our payment processing delays. This relationship needs
nurturing to ensure continued priority treatment.

================================================================================
RECOMMENDATIONS
================================================================================

1. Schedule executive risk review for integration workstream
2. Consider temporary change freeze while CCB catches up
3. Mandate knowledge transfer documentation for all critical-path resources
4. PM to schedule vendor relationship meeting in early January
